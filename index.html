Career

Christian Huacón is an undergraduate Electrical Engineering Student at the City College of New York, City University of New York .

While pursuing his undergraduate degree, Christian has been participating in research programs such as the Collegiate Science and Technology Entry Program (CSTEP) at Hostos Community College, NSF REU Program in Data Science at Worcester Polytechnic Institute, The Summer Undergraduate Research in Engineering/Sciences (S.U.R.E.) program at Georgia Institute of Technology and NOAA Center for Earth System Sciences & Remote Sensing Technologies at the City College of New York, in the areas of IoT, Data Science, Web Development and Robotics.

In 2018 at Georgia Institute of Technology, a Magnet-Wheeled Climbing Mobile Sensor for Steel Structures was developed. Wireless mobile sensors have been developed and utilized in structural health monitoring. The developed wireless mobile sensor can navigate to targeted locations and measure the acceleration response of steel structures. The advanced wireless mobile sensor was designed to be capable of detecting hazards during navigation and exciting the structure with a hammer-controlled system.

In 2017 at Worcester Polytechnic Institute, the idea of increasingly widespread use of embedded sensor technology within cities became important to the research community because it was observed and abundance of heterogeneous spatio-temporal data, such as vehicle trajectories, points of interests, etc. The data visualization and analysis became a challenge for more effective urban planning. The purpose of this project was to develop SURV, a Smart URban Visualization system.

In 2016 at Hostos Community College due to the advancement in technology and increase in economic growth, environmental pollution became rampant throughout metropolitan cities, therefore, it was a challenge that needed to be addressed. The purpose of this research was based on designing a low-cost, cloud-based smart device named Cloud-based Environment Monitoring Smart Device (CEMSD) that monitors different environmental parameters such as air quality, noise, temperature and humidity.
